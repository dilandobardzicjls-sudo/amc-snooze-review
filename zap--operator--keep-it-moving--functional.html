The work stops the moment you step away

You kick off an agent, and for a while it doesn’t need you. Then it stops (one small question, one “shall I keep going?”) and everything waits until you’re back at the computer.

With Zap, that answer costs one press. Your ready-made “yes, keep going” sits on the little ⚡ in the reply bar. Tap it, or hit Alt+Z at the desk, and it’s sent in under a second.

People without it describe an agent frozen 15 minutes for a one-word yes. With Zap, the answer is already written. The wait is one press long.

Try it from the couch

  1. An agent pauses to ask. The session lands in your inbox, flagged “Needs you.”
  2. Open it anywhere, at the desk or from the couch.
  3. Tap Zap. Your preset “yes, keep going” sends instantly; the agent resumes.

Zap rides the same reply bar in sessions, texts, and email, and attachments come along. Want it to say something else? Right-click the ⚡ and change the words, even a couple of lines. At the desk, Alt+Z presses it for you: one keystroke, no typing.
