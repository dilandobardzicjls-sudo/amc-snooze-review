Classification, not vibes

70 fixed patterns (69 high-confidence, 1 medium) + guard rules read every turn-end and decide one thing: is this reply waiting on you, or on its own work? Guards run first and win every conflict. Anything that reads as a question, an approval directed at you, a policy block, or any wait aimed at you surfaces. The detector never answers for you, full stop.

Waits on its own work stay Running. If a job is attached (a background command, a cloud run, a monitor, a subagent, a scheduled wake-up), the finish wakes the agent, pass or fail. Event-driven, not a keep-alive timer burning turns. A prose wait with no job behind it doesn’t pretend: it holds, then surfaces at your window.

Rule-based, not model-judged: hover any ⏱ tag and see the rule that fired and the phrase it matched. Model-free by default. An opt-in AI second opinion exists, and the rule verdict is always the floor. Pattern changes ship only after backtesting against tens of thousands of real transcript messages with zero false holds.

Keep your notify hooks if you like them. This is the half they can’t do: waking the agent instead of you.

Bounded, on the record

A silent wait never outlives your window (30 minutes by default, 5-240), then it surfaces bright orange with what it was waiting on. That includes a job-backed wait whose event never comes: a hung build hits the same window and surfaces the same way.

Worst case, stated plainly: a misread question waits one window, then reaches you with a receipt. That’s the whole trade, and you size it.

When a wait surfaces, Keep waiting re-arms one more window of its own length (60 minutes by default) and Check in pokes the agent instead, both in the chat and the inbox right-click menu.

And classification only ever decides when you get pinged, never what the agent is allowed to do. Permissions and approvals are untouched.

Your knobs

  1. On by default since 2026-05-27. Hover any ⏱ tag for the rule + matched phrase.
  2. Waiting period: Settings → Sessions. 30 min default, 5-240. Keep waiting has its own length: 60 min default, 5-240.
  3. Auto check-in (opt-in, off): when the window ends, AMC pokes the agent for you, at most 3× in a row, each visible in the chat as “Checked in with the agent (N of 3).”
  4. Strict mode: turn off “Recognize all waiting phrasings” and only the canonical waiting sentence suppresses. Arbitrary prose never does.
  5. Escape hatches: AMC_DISABLE_WAITING_DETECTOR=1 kills the detector, AMC_DISABLE_AUTO_NUDGE=1 kills check-ins. And the detector never overrides a tool-scheduled deferral.
